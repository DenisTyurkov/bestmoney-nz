Factors like your suburb, car model, and claims history significantly impact your premium." - question: "What affects my car insurance premium?" answer: "Key factors include: your age and driving experience, the car's make, model and age, where you live and park, your claims history, how much you drive annually, your chosen excess amount, and any safety features on your vehicle. Young drivers and those in high-theft areas typically pay more." - question: "Should I choose agreed value or market value?" answer: "Agreed value locks in a payout amount when you take out the policy - you know exactly what you'll receive if your car is written off. Market value pays what your car is worth at claim time, which depreciates over time. Agreed value costs slightly more but provides certainty, especially important for newer vehicles." - question: "What's the difference between excess and premium?" answer: "Your premium is the regular amount you pay for insurance (monthly or annually). Your excess is what you pay out of pocket when making a claim. Choosing a higher excess typically lowers your premium, but means you pay more if you need to claim. Consider what you can afford at claim time." - question: "How do I make a car insurance claim in NZ?" answer: "Most insurers allow claims via phone, online, or through their app. You'll need to provide details of the incident, photos of damage, a police report (for theft or hit and run), and details of any other parties involved. Claims are typically assessed within a few days, though complex cases may take longer." ---
